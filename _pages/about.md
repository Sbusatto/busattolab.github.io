## Welcome to the Busatto LAB

The Biological Nanoparticles Laboratory is located within the Nanomedicine and Drug Delivery Department at the Groningen Research Institute of Pharmacy (University of Groningen). 

Led by Dr. Sara Busatto, our lab focuses on the isolation, characterization, loading, and engineering of biological cell-derived nanoparticles, with a specific emphasis on **extracellular vesicles (EVs)**. We utilize multidisciplinary approaches combining molecular biology, nanotechnology, and biophysics to understand the role of EVs in oncology and to develop novel nanotherapeutics.

---

### Our Core Focus
Our independent research lines are currently centered on three main pillars:

* **Breast to Brain Metastasis:** Investigating the role of cancer-derived EVs in the very early steps of metastasis, particularly how they prepare a brain microenvironment (pre-metastatic niche) that favors the survival and growth of metastatic cells.
* **Biocompatible Drug Delivery:** Developing novel, scalable methods for loading and engineering EV-mimetics to create actively targeted delivery vehicles for primary and metastatic breast cancer.
* **The Biomolecular Corona:** Studying the formation, composition, and functional impact of the biomolecular corona on cancer-derived EVs.
