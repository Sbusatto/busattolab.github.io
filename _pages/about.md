  <h4 style="font-weight: 700; margin-bottom: 20px;">Decoding Extracellular Vesicles to Defeat Metastasis</h4>
  
  <p style="font-size: 1.1em; line-height: 1.6; color: var(--global-text-color);">
    Welcome to the <strong>Biological Nanoparticles Laboratory</strong>. We are an independent research group within the <strong>Nanomedicine and Drug Delivery Department</strong> at the Groningen Research Institute of Pharmacy (University of Groningen). Led by <strong>Dr. Sara Busatto</strong>—following her tenure at <strong>Harvard Medical School</strong> and <strong>Boston Children’s Hospital</strong>—our lab focuses on the intersection of biological nanoparticles and cancer progression. We investigate the critical role of <strong>Extracellular Vesicles (EVs)</strong> in driving <strong>Breast-to-Brain Metastasis</strong> and leverage these insights to engineer targeted, next-generation <strong>EV-mimetic therapeutics</strong>.
  </p>

        <h5 style="color: #E3000F;"><b>Breast to Brain Metastasis</b></h5>
        <p style="font-size: 0.95em; font-weight: 600; margin-bottom: 10px;">Investigating the role of cancer-derived EVs in the early steps of metastasis.</p>
        <p style="font-size: 0.9em; margin-bottom: 10px;">Our laboratory investigates the role of cancer-derived extracellular vesicles (EVs) in the very early steps that lead to the formation of breast to brain metastasis.</p>
        <p style="font-size: 0.9em; margin-bottom: 0;">In particular, we are focused on how the preparation of a brain microenvironment—better known as the pre-metastatic niche—is influenced by EVs to favor the survival and growth of metastatic cells.</p>

        <h5 style="color: #E3000F;"><b>EV-Mimetics & Drug Delivery</b></h5>
        <p style="font-size: 0.95em; font-weight: 600; margin-bottom: 10px;">Loading and engineering EV-mimetics as biocompatible targeted vehicles.</p>
        <p style="font-size: 0.9em; margin-bottom: 10px;">We are developing novel and scalable engineering methods applicable to EVs and EV-mimetics to create actively targeted drug delivery vehicles.</p>
        <p style="font-size: 0.9em; margin-bottom: 0;">Our proprietary methods allow for the scalable and rapid synthesis of cell-membrane-derived EV-mimicking nanoparticles. We aim to utilize these vehicles to deliver small non-coding genetic material, proteins, and standard chemotherapies directly to primary and metastatic breast cancer cells.</p>
      </div>

        <h5 style="color: #E3000F;"><b>The Biomolecular Corona</b></h5>
        <p style="font-size: 0.95em; font-weight: 600; margin-bottom: 10px;">Studying the formation and composition of the biomolecular corona of cancer-derived EVs.</p>
        <p style="font-size: 0.9em; margin-bottom: 10px;">Nanoparticles introduced into biological fluids rapidly acquire a coating of biomolecules, known as the biomolecular corona.</p>
        <p style="font-size: 0.9em; margin-bottom: 10px;">Our laboratory studies the formation, composition, and functional impact of this biomolecular corona on cancer-derived EVs. Understanding these interactions is critical for elucidating the physiological role of EVs and for optimizing them as therapeutic delivery systems.</p>
        <p style="font-size: 0.85em; margin-bottom: 0; font-style: italic; color: var(--global-text-color-light);">We also contribute to collaborative projects on the role of EVs in Parkinson's disease and the use of EV-mimetics as vehicles for the delivery of protein-based therapeutics.</p>
